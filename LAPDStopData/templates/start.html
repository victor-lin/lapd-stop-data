Los Angeles Police Department Stop Data

Welcome to the LAPD Traffic Stop database! This website provides an in-depth insight into traffic stops in the city of Los Angeles, California in 2015. Read more about how and why we chose this project below.

Our Method

The Los Angeles Police Department’s traffic stop data from 2015 is an open source dataset obtained from the internet. The data, consisting over a million records, covers information about every traffic stop, including the officer and the offender*. From this data, we generated graphical representations of information that is most persistent to current day issues in society. Charts highlighting arrests by location and civilian demographic aim to provide a deeper insight into each civilian/officer interaction.

Our Goal

The goal of our website is to provide eye-opening information regarding police/civilian interaction in one of America’s most populated cities, Los Angeles. In the past year, social tensions have risen between civilians and officers, resulting in nationwide movements, such as the Black Lives Matter and Blue Lives Matter movements. We aim to provide a factual and unbiased representation of the Los Angeles Police Department’s traffic stop data to explain the recent trend in these movements.
